Map of Gentrification in Brooklyn

In a lab assignment for my Critical GIS class, we applied Governing.com's methodology of identifying gentrified Census tracts to Brooklyn. In this GIS practice, we analyzed only tracts in Brooklyn rather than the greater New York metropolitan area. Please note that this map does not fully measure gentrification. It is an exercise in using urban data to come to one of many understandings about a place. Our class used Census 2000 and 2010 data to analyze population, income, home value, and educational attainment data. To make this map, I used a combination of ESRI ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator. This poster is designed for educational purposes for my Critical GIS class.
