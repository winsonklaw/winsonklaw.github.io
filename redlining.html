The Realities of Redlining in Brooklyn

In a lab assignment for my Critical GIS class, we used data from the Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) of 1975 to map the percentage of approved home loans in Census tracts in Brooklyn, NY. We then compared these percentages to the percentage of African Americans in those same Census tracts. It turns out that the tracts with a greater percentage of African Americans receive fewer home loans compared to tracts with a smaller percentage of African Americans. To make this map, I used a combination of ESRI ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator. This poster is designed for educational purposes for my Critical GIS class.
