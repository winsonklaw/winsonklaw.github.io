Winson Law

Mapping Visible Gentrification in Seattle's Central District

In an independent project for my Critical GIS class, I applied Hwang and Sampson's (2014) methodology of coding visible changes in Chicago block faces to the case of Seattle. In this GIS practice, I used Google Street View to look at changes in Seattle's historically black Central District neighborhood between 2007 and 2015. I scored block faces along E Union St in the Central District based on signs of public reinvestment, large-scale developments, and other visible changes. To make this map, I used a combination of ESRI ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator and InDesign. This poster is designed for educational purposes for my Critical GIS class.
