How Does Instagram Produce an Image of a Place?

In a lab assignment for my Critical GIS class, we placed public geo-coded Instagram photos in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, NY. In this GIS practice, we developed personal categories for what "hipster" means, tagged each photo within Williamsburg if it fit a category of "hipster," and put them on a Google Map. My categories included indie music, food, and nightlife. The goal of the lab was to look at how social media can play a strong role in creating an image of a place and, in turn, drive gentrification processes. To make this map, I used a combination of ESRI ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator. This poster is designed for educational purposes for my Critical GIS class.
