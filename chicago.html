Map of Underlying Causes of Death in Chicago

In my cartographic design class, I used Chicago Data Portal data on selected underlying causes of death and Census block data to create maps of causes of death by racial enclaves. To make this map, I used a combination of ESRI ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator and InDesign. I first created racial enclaves in Chicago by combining Census blocks by adjacency and majority race. I then performed a weighted reaggregation analysis to summarize the underlying causes of death for each of the racial enclaves. Finally, I created maps that summarized these findings to capture audience attention. This poster is designed for print and display in the Middlebury Geography department.
